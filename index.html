    Pull Day 1 Monday · Heavy Back

    Pull▼
    Standing Straight Bar or Rope Pullover
    3 sets × 12–15 reps · Same weight all sets
    Hit 15 reps before increasing weight. Full stretch at top, hard squeeze at bottom. Keep it in the lats — don't let triceps take over.
    Single Arm Lat Pulldown
    Top set: 10–12 reps each side · Back-off: 12–15 reps each side
    Set incline bench in front of machine for optimal angle. Warm up sets = activation only. Full stretch, constant tension, squeeze hard driving into lower lat. Neutral grip, elbow tucked close to body.
    Underhand Bent Over Barbell Row
    Top set: 8–12 reps · Back-off: 10–15 reps · 2–3 min rest
    Stay in position — don't posture up or it becomes shrugs. Emulate Dorian Yates row technique. Underhand grip targets lower lats. Builds overall back thickness and density.
    Chest Supported Machine Row
    Top set: 8–10 reps · Back-off: 12–15 reps · Nothing below double digits
    Let shoulders roll forward, round around pad for full stretch, then retract and drive elbows back. Lights up mid and upper back.
    Deadlifts
    2 working sets × 10–12 reps · 2–3 min rest
    Maximum tension and complete control. Progress strength deliberately over time.
    Bodyweight or Machine Assisted Pullups
    3 sets to failure
    Use band or assisted machine if needed. Build reps over time.
    Abs — End of Session
    Hanging knee or leg raises
    Planks — 1–2 min hold
    Traditional sit-ups — no arm swinging
    2 rounds × 15–20+ reps · 60 sec rest between rounds

    Push Day 1 Tuesday · Heavy Chest

    Push▼
    Cable Chest Press
    2 sets × 12–15 reps
    Warm up thoroughly first. True failure on last set only — purpose is to prime the chest. Trap pinned down, shoulders back, arms slightly bent, feed bicep into pec.
    Incline DB Press or Smith Machine Low Incline
    Top set: 8–12 reps · Back-off: 10–15 reps · 2–3 min rest
    Elbows at 60 degrees, full active range of motion, solid squeeze at top. Work up to very hard top set then drop 10–20% for back-off.
    Flat Chest Press — Machine or Plate Loaded
    Top set: 10–12 reps · Back-off: 12–15 reps · 2–3 min rest
    Find a machine you connect with for stability. Avoid chest caving, keep front delts from taking over.
    Pec Dec Flys
    3 sets × 12–15 reps to failure
    Finisher. Challenging weight but form holds. Goal is maximum blood in the chest.
    Cable or Machine Lateral Raises
    2 sets × 15–20 reps · 1–2 warm up sets first
    Strict form only. Slight lean forward, shoulders back, arm straight, hold at top, fight the negative. Use a cuff if available.

    Legs Thursday · Heavy Legs

    Legs▼
    Seated Hamstring Curls
    Top set: 10–12 reps · Back-off: 12–15 reps
    Wait 20–30 sec after back-off, cut weight in half, controlled set to failure. Forces blood into hamstrings to warm up knees before squatting.
    Adductors
    1–2 feeder sets · 2 working sets × 15–18 reps to complete failure
    Contraction is everything. If you're not walking funny after — you didn't do it right.
    Hack or Smith Machine Squats
    Top set: 8–12 reps · Back-off: 10–15 reps · 2.5–3 min rest
    Feet slightly inside or at shoulder width, toes slightly out. Hamstrings to calves for full range of motion. Top set — leave nothing on the table.
    Leg Press
    3 sets × 15–20 reps, each pushing close to failure
    If you can't hit 15 for all 3, weight is too heavy. Feet outside shoulder width, as low on pad as possible without heels coming up. Load the quads.
    Walking DB Lunges or Bulgarian Split Squat
    2 sets × 10–15 reps · Both steps = 1 rep · 1–2 min rest
    Slight forward lean for quad emphasis. Lead leg does all the work. 80–90% max weight, controlled contractions.

    Pull Day 2 Friday · Medium Back

    Pull▼
    Traditional Grip Lat Pulldown
    Top set: 10–12 reps · Back-off: 12–15 reps
    No leaning back — stay neutral, chest up, elbows drive straight down. Activation only on warm up sets.
    Seated Wide Grip Row
    3 sets × 10–15 reps
    Shoulders pinned down, elbows wide, pull bar to sternum. Light up mid and upper back.
    Chest Supported T-Bar Row
    Top set: 10–15 reps · Back-off: 10–15 reps · Upper back & trap focus
    Round back around pad on the stretch. Elbows tucked and driven back as far as possible while chest stays on pad.
    Bent Over DB Row
    Top set: 10–12 reps each side · Back-off: 12–15 reps · 1–1.5 min rest
    60–75 degree angle, 90% max load with perfect form. Chin tucked, spine neutral. Drive elbow toward hip — not straight up and down.
    Bodyweight Pullups
    3 sets to failure
    Use band or assisted machine if needed. Build reps over time.
    Abs — End of Session
    Hanging knee or leg raises
    Planks — 1–2 min hold
    Traditional sit-ups — no arm swinging
    2 rounds × 15–20+ reps · 60 sec rest between rounds

    Push Day 2 Saturday · Medium Shoulders

    Push▼
    Single or Double Arm Reverse Pec Dec — Rear Delt
    3 sets × 12–15 reps · Last set to failure
    Finesse movement. Squeeze and stretch every rep. Traps pinned, keep mid back and scapula from taking over.
    Seated DB or Machine Shoulder Press
    Top set: 8–12 reps · Back-off: 10–15 reps · 1.5–2 min rest
    Elbows at 45 degrees, pointed toward the ground — do not let them flare back.
    DB Lateral Raise
    Set 1: 15–20 reps (light) · Set 2: 15 reps (moderate) · Sets 3 & 4: heavy to failure + drop set
    Immediately drop set after sets 3 & 4 — cut weight in half, to failure again. Arms 90% straight, chest up, shoulders back, traps out of it.
    Incline Chest Press Machine
    Top set: 10–12 reps · Back-off: 12–15 reps
    Controlled movement, no jerking. Squeeze at top. Elbows tucked, feed bicep into pec.
    Single Arm Cable Lateral Raise superset w/ Rope Face Pulls
    3 sets × 15 reps · Last set to failure
    First 2 sets leave 1–2 reps in reserve.

    Arm Day Bonus · Bi/Tri Supersets

    Arms▼
    Warmup — Light DB Curls + Standing DB Tricep Extension
    2 sets · stretch & activation
    Light weight, full range. Warm the biceps with controlled curls; stretch the long head of the triceps with a slow standing extension. Prime the joints, no fatigue.
    Alt. DB Curls superset w/ Lying Barbell Skull Crushers
    3 sets pyramiding — warmup, feeder, top set · Top set: 8–10 reps
    Increment weight every set. Alternate arms on curls — full supination, squeeze hard at top. On skull crushers, lower bar behind the head, drive through the long head of the tricep.
    Barbell Curls superset w/ Lying DB Skull Crushers
    3 sets pyramiding · Top set: 8–10 reps
    Keep elbows pinned at sides on barbell curls — no swinging. DB skulls allow neutral grip and deeper stretch than barbell version. Control the negative.
    Single-Arm Preacher Curl superset w/ Bent-Over Tricep Extensions
    3 sets × 10–12 reps each arm
    Right arm first, then left — both arms get full 10–12 reps. Slow eccentric. Bent-over tri ext: hinge at hips, lock the upper arm, extend through the elbow only — feel the long head light up.
    Cable Curls superset w/ Tricep Pulldowns
    3 sets × 12–15 reps · Finisher — chase the pump
    Constant tension throughout. Last set to failure. Goal is maximum blood in the arms — let the cables do their job at the end-range stretch and squeeze.

    Reduce plastic food storage when possible
    Avoid prolonged contact of acidic foods with plastic

    My Covenant
    April 10, 2026 — Day 1
    "I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me."
    — Philippians 4:13

    I am doing this for God first, my family second, and the world third. Not for the mirror. Not for the approval of others. Not to prove anything to anyone who doubted me or to reclaim something I lost. Those were the reasons before. Those reasons always ran out.

    This time the foundation is different. This time it is built on something that cannot be shaken.

    I have been given a body, a mind, and a spirit. They are not mine — they were entrusted to me. My body is a temple of the Holy Spirit and I will treat it accordingly. Not with obsession, but with stewardship. With discipline born from gratitude, not from fear. I will fuel it with precision, train it with intention, and rest it without guilt. I will do this not because I have to, but because I choose to honor what God gave me — for the rest of my life.

    I have been given a mind. I will guard it. I will feed it with truth, with wisdom, with the Word. I will protect my peace the way I protect my family — fiercely and deliberately. I will not allow the noise of this world — its fear, its comparison, its chaos — to live rent free in the mind God gave me.

    I have been given a spirit. And I know now, after everything, that when my spirit is empty nothing else fills. Not achievement. Not a number on a scale. Not a physique. I have chased all of those and they were good but they were not enough. Christ is enough. His presence is the foundation I was always trying to build on top of sand before.

    I am almost 40. I am a husband. I am a father. I am a man who left comfort for purpose, who bet on himself in California when most could not. I carry that responsibility not as a burden but as a calling. The people who look up to me — I do not take that lightly. The light God placed in me is not for me alone. It is meant to be seen. And the best way I can share it is by simply becoming the fullest version of the man He designed me to be.

    I have had glimpses of that man. At 194 pounds with tears in my eyes. In the quiet after the chaos of Vegas. In moments with my wife and child where I knew, deeply, that this life is worth protecting.

    I am not chasing a transformation anymore. I am walking into one. With open hands, with faith, with structure and grace in equal measure. Knowing I will be imperfect. Knowing God's mercies are new every morning. Knowing that the goal is not perfection — it is faithfulness. Showing up. Every day. To the meal. To the training. To the Word. To my family. To the calling. To take ACTION.

    Perfect is good, done is better. Done is better, faithfulness is best. My motto has always been to take action and to get it done — it got me through a lot, but now it is to take action — IN FAITH knowing that in times of trial, I don't have to do this alone. I have Him. Always.

    "I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me."
    This is my time. And I am ready.
